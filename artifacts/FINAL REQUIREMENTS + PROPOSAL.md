FINAL REQUIREMENTS DOCUMENT v2
Merged baseline including the ratings, suggestions/tips, and map-based social-support extension
Merge summary. This version preserves REQ-001 to REQ-118 and REQ-121 to REQ-137 from the approved baseline, refines REQ-119, REQ-120, and REQ-129, and adds REQ-138 to REQ-144 for ratings, suggestions/tips, place-context association, map-based discovery, and related privacy and moderation controls.
1. Final Assumptions
A1. ErasmusMate is a mobility management platform with two layers: a primary institutional/manual-assisted layer for official Erasmus-style procedures and a secondary social-support layer for Erasmus-context student support.
A2. The primary operational roles are Student, Coordinator, and Administrator.
A3. Each active mobility process is managed within a specific mobility record linked to a student.
A4. Official mobility management spans pre-departure, during-mobility, and end-of-mobility phases.
A5. Applicable procedures, documents, deadlines, review responsibilities, and validations may vary by institution, destination, mobility type, and lifecycle phase.
A6. Some official procedures require document submission, some require review and decision, and some require both.
A7. Some official documents require one or more signatures from defined institutional participants.
A8. Coordinators remain responsible for manual review, approval, rejection, reopening, and exception handling even when the system performs validations and status control.
A9. Exceptions, deadline extensions, and resubmissions are part of the core official mobility scope.
A10. Official notifications are system-generated communications related to deadlines, submissions, decisions, exceptions, signatures, and operational failures.
A11. Social-support functions are Erasmus-contextual and are intended to complement, not replace, institutional mobility management.
A12. Social-support features are available only to eligible users within the permitted Erasmus context and are subject to consent, visibility, moderation, and lifecycle controls.
A13. Social connections, discovery visibility, and messaging permissions may depend on destination, institution, mobility period, lifecycle stage, and privacy settings.
A14. Administrators are responsible for governance and moderation of user-generated social-support content and user-safety incidents when such features are enabled.
A15. External systems may provide identity, student and academic reference data, document storage, notification delivery, and e-signature capabilities.
A16. The updated brief is the primary source of truth; prior baseline and expanded proposals are controlled supporting context only.
A17. Ratings, suggestions/tips, and map-based discovery are limited to Erasmus-relevant user-generated social content and are intended to complement, not replace, official mobility management.
A18. Map support is limited to exploration of accessible place-contextual social content and does not include official procedure navigation, route planning, or real-time user tracking.
A19. Existing social-layer controls for eligibility, visibility, consent, moderation, reporting, and feature scoping continue to apply to ratings, tips, and mapped content unless this baseline states a more specific rule.
A20. Map-linked social content is associated only with supported Erasmus-relevant place contexts and must not depend on exposing a user’s precise personal location.
2. Final Constraints
C1. The updated brief prevails over all prior baseline, seed, or review material in case of conflict.
C2. This baseline contains only assumptions, constraints, functional requirements, and scope notes.
C3. Business rules, workflows, acceptance criteria, architecture, APIs, and database design are out of scope at this stage.
C4. Functional requirements shall be implementation-agnostic except where external integration boundaries must be stated.
C5. The institutional/manual-assisted mobility-management core shall remain primary and shall be preserved unless a correction is necessary for consistency with the updated brief.
C6. The social-support layer is in scope in this iteration, but it shall remain clearly secondary to the institutional core.
C7. Social-support requirements shall be limited to Erasmus-relevant matching, connection lifecycle, messaging, recommendations, opinions, moderation, reporting, blocking, search/discovery, and privacy/visibility controls.
C8. Leisure-only planning, open-ended social networking, open anonymous community interaction, automated group channels, and non-Erasmus lifestyle features are out of scope.
C9. Requirements shall be specific, independently testable, non-duplicated, and suitable to serve as a baseline for later derivation of business rules, workflows, domain model, and acceptance criteria.
C10. Each functional requirement text shall start with a coverage prefix in the form [Coverage Area].
C11. Coverage shall remain balanced across roles, lifecycle phases, normal and exception flows, institutional controls, and social-support governance.
C12. Stable requirement IDs shall be preserved where possible; new requirements shall be appended sequentially unless merge or retirement is necessary for correctness.
C13. New requirements shall be added only where they materially improve domain coverage, moderation value, privacy protection, lifecycle clarity, product differentiation, or traceability.
C14. Institutional requirements and social-support requirements shall remain conceptually distinguishable and shall not distort the official lifecycle, permissions, or compliance model.
C15. The requirements baseline includes a focused social-support extension for ratings, suggestions/tips, map-based discovery, and the minimum related privacy, moderation, and safety controls needed to make that extension complete and testable.
C16. Map-based social discovery shall remain clearly separate from official procedure navigation and official institutional record search.
C17. The requirements baseline shall remain implementation-agnostic and shall not introduce GIS technology choices, UI mockups, APIs, database design, workflows, business rules, domain model elements, or architecture through this extension.
C18. The requirements baseline shall not introduce generic social feeds, leisure-only tourism planning, full navigation or route planning, real-time geolocation tracking, or public exposure of exact personal addresses or sensitive private locations.
C19. Requirements added or refined by this extension shall remain specific, independently testable, non-duplicated, and limited to material enrichment of the approved social-support layer.

4. Scope Notes
institutional_core
The institutional core remains unchanged and includes identity, role-based access, mobility records, official procedure catalog management, procedure versioning and publication, lifecycle and status control, document submission and validation, required signers and signatures, deadlines and exceptions, official notifications, coordinator review and delegation, auditability, integrations, privacy/security, accessibility, and operational reliability.
social_support
The social-support layer includes eligible student profiles, privacy-controlled discoverability, Erasmus-context student matching, connection requests and lifecycle, mutual-opt-in messaging, recommendations, suggestions or practical tips, opinions and reviews with ratings, favorites, reporting, blocking, moderation actions, consent controls, social notification preferences, social audit traceability, place-context association for accessible content, map-based social discovery, map filtering, preview-to-detail exploration, and reportability of mapped social-support content.
explicitly_out_of_scope
The following remain explicitly out of scope for this baseline: leisure-only features, open-ended social networking, anonymous community interaction, automated group channels or group communities, route planning, real-time user tracking, arbitrary user-defined map points, exposure of exact personal or sensitive private locations, social functions unrelated to Erasmus context, workflows, business rules, acceptance criteria, architecture, APIs, and database design.
5. Merge Note
This merged baseline supersedes the prior requirements baseline and incorporates the approved ratings, suggestions/tips, and map-based social-support extension as part of the official ErasmusMate requirements catalog.
